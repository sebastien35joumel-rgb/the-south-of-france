Grasse, the Town of Perfume

The perfume capital of the world in the hills above Cannes — jasmine and rose fields, century-old houses, and a craft that turned a tanning town into scent. Grasse, on the Côte d’Azur.

By The Editors — Grasse, Alpes-Maritimes
Spring 20268 min read Share LinkMail

Grasse stands in the hills behind Cannes and has, for three hundred years, been the perfume capital of the world. It began as a town of tanners; when scented gloves came into fashion the tanners turned to scent, the mild climate gave them the flowers, and the trade has never left. The whole craft of perfumery, UNESCO now agrees, is kept alive here.

I. The town that smells of flowers

The old town climbs in steep stepped lanes of warm stone, and three of the great houses — Fragonard, Galimard and Molinard — open their doors to show how it is done, from the flowers to the still. The Musée International de la Parfumerie tells the longer story; everywhere the air carries something.

“A town of tanners that learned to bottle the spring.”

From the notebook

II. The flowers and the fields

The scents begin in the fields around the town — the rose de mai picked in May, the jasmine in late summer at dawn before the heat, the tuberose and orange blossom that go into the great perfumes. A handful of growers still supply the famous houses by name; the jasmine of Grasse remains one of the most precious raw materials in perfumery.

III. Fragonard’s town

Grasse also gave France a painter — Jean-Honoré Fragonard, born here in 1732, whose light touch is shown in the museum that keeps his name. Between the perfume houses and the galleries, the town makes an easy day from the coast, and a cooler one: it sits high enough to escape the August heat of Cannes below.

Plan the visit

Getting there
About 40 min from Cannes or Nice by car or bus, up into the hills.
When to go
May for the rose, August dawns for the jasmine; spring and autumn for the town itself.
Where to stay
La Bastide Saint-Antoine, an old olive estate turned hotel, with the coast below.
