# Saint-Jean-Cap-Ferrat and the Villa Ephrussi — The South of France
> A green peninsula of legendary villas between Nice and Monaco, a rose-pink palace ringed by nine gardens, and a coast path round the whole cape. Cap-Ferrat, on the Côte d’Azur.

Between Nice and Monaco a wooded peninsula reaches out into the sea — **Cap-Ferrat**, for a century one of the most coveted addresses on earth. Kings and writers built villas in its pines; Somerset Maugham wrote here, and a Rothschild raised a palace of rose-pink stone above two bays. And yet a public path runs the whole shore, free to anyone.

## I. The cape and the coast path

The **sentier du littoral** rounds the entire peninsula at the water’s edge — past the villas hidden in their gardens, the little **Plage de Paloma**, the lighthouse at the point, and back to the fishing harbour of **Saint-Jean**. It is one of the loveliest coastal walks on the Riviera, and it asks nothing but good shoes and a couple of hours.

“The villas are private, but the path along the sea belongs to everyone.”

## II. The Villa Ephrussi de Rothschild

On the narrow neck of the cape stands the **Villa Ephrussi de Rothschild**, the rose-pink belle-époque palace Béatrice de Rothschild filled with art and surrounded with **nine gardens** — French, Spanish, Florentine, Japanese, a garden of stone and a garden of roses — laid out so the whole thing reads, from above, like a ship between two blue bays. The musical fountains play on the hour.

![Formal gardens running down to a blue bay](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1582497475588-49b76871cf42?fm=jpg&q=76&w=2400&auto=format&fit=crop)

The gardens of the Villa Ephrussi — nine worlds between Villefranche and Beaulieu.

## III. The harbour and around

The village of **Saint-Jean** keeps a real fishing harbour turned gently to pleasure, with restaurants round the quay and the **Grand-Hôtel du Cap-Ferrat** on the point. Next door, **Villefranche-sur-Mer** and **Beaulieu** with its Greek **Villa Kérylos** complete one of the richest few kilometres of coast in France.

### Plan the _visit_

Getting there

About 20 min from Nice by car or bus; the nearest train is at **Beaulieu-sur-Mer**, a short walk on.

When to go

Spring and autumn; the coast path is best in the morning, the gardens for the fountains on the hour.

Where to stay

**Grand-Hôtel du Cap-Ferrat** on the point, or a small hotel by the harbour of Saint-Jean.
