# The Pont du Gard and Roman Nîmes — The South of France
> The greatest Roman aqueduct still standing, three tiers of golden arches over a river, and the city it was built to water. The Pont du Gard and Nîmes, in the Gard.

The **Pont du Gard** carries no road and no water now, and it is still one of the most astonishing things the Romans left behind — three tiers of arches in honey-gold stone, fifty metres above the **Gardon**, built in the first century to step an aqueduct across the valley on its fifty-kilometre run to **Nîmes**.

## I. The bridge over the Gardon

It was raised without mortar, the blocks cut so true they have held for two thousand years; some still carry the masons’ marks and the jutting stones left for the scaffolding. Come early or late and walk both banks — the scale only lands when a person crosses beneath the lowest arch and shrinks to nothing. In summer the river below is for swimming.

“Two thousand years, no mortar, and not a stone has moved.”

## II. Nîmes, the most Roman city in France

The water ran to **Nîmes**, ancient Nemausus, which keeps more of Rome than any city in France. The **Arènes**, an amphitheatre of the first century, is among the best preserved anywhere and still fills for events; the **Maison Carrée**, a near-perfect temple, stands in the middle of town as it has for two millennia, now faced by a modern gallery across the square.

![A Roman amphitheatre in a southern French city](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1526822162485-79f02ba82a9d?fm=jpg&q=76&w=2400&auto=format&fit=crop)

The Arènes of Nîmes — a first-century amphitheatre, still in use.

## III. Gardens, denim and the road on

Behind the temple, the **Jardins de la Fontaine** were laid out in the eighteenth century around the Roman spring, climbing to the **Tour Magne** for the view over the rooftops. Nîmes also gave the world a word it never asks for credit on: the sturdy cloth _serge de Nîmes_ — **denim**. From here the Languedoc opens west, toward the coast and the canal.

### Plan the _visit_

Getting there

Nîmes is on the TGV; the Pont du Gard is about 25 min north by car or seasonal bus.

When to go

Spring and autumn for the city; early or late at the bridge to have it nearly to yourself.

Where to stay

**Maison Albar — L’Imperator**, by the gardens, long the grande dame of Nîmes.
