Marseille was founded by Greek sailors twenty-six centuries ago, and it has never stopped being a port — loud, salt-stained, impossible to tidy and entirely itself. The **Vieux-Port** is still its heart, a rectangle of water where the fishing boats land and the whole city seems to pass by before lunch.

## I. The Old Port and the morning fish

At the quay’s head the **fish market** sells the morning’s catch straight off the boats — rascasse and conger for the **bouillabaisse**, the city’s great saffron-and-rockfish stew. Above, a mirror canopy by Norman Foster reflects the crowd upside down; behind, the cafés and the long climb of the city begin.

“A port first and a city second — and it would not have it any other way.”

## II. Le Panier and the MuCEM

North of the port, **Le Panier** is the oldest quarter, a hill of washing-lined lanes and painted shutters climbing to the **Vieille Charité**. At the harbour mouth the **MuCEM** — the museum of Mediterranean civilisations — stands in a black concrete lattice linked by a footbridge to the old **Fort Saint-Jean**, the new city shaking hands with the old.

![A modern museum in dark lattice beside an old fort](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1614888773798-df30e12a9b6c?fm=jpg&q=76&w=2400&auto=format&fit=crop)

The MuCEM and Fort Saint-Jean — the city’s two ages at the water’s edge.

## III. Notre-Dame and the sea

Over everything stands **Notre-Dame de la Garde**, the gold Virgin on the highest hill, watched by every sailor leaving the port and the whole city laid out at her feet. From the **Corniche** below, the road runs south to the fishing cove of the **Vallon des Auffes** and on toward the white cliffs of the Calanques.

### Plan the _visit_

Getting there

Marseille-Saint-Charles is 3 h from Paris by TGV; the centre and port are a short metro or walk away.

When to go

Spring and autumn; the Vieux-Port is best very early, with the fish and the light.

Where to stay

**Hôtel C2** or **InterContinental — Hôtel-Dieu**, above the port, for the view down to the water.
