The Gorges du Verdon

The deepest canyon in Europe, a green-turquoise river a thousand feet below the road, and a lake the colour of glass at its mouth. The Verdon, between Provence and the Alps.

By The Editors — La Palud-sur-Verdon
Spring 20268 min read Share LinkMail

The Verdon has cut a canyon nearly seven hundred metres deep into the limestone of upper Provence — the grandest gorge in Europe, and the strangest colour. The river runs an unreal green-turquoise from the glacial melt and the minerals in the rock, far enough below the rim that from the road it looks painted on.

I. The road along the rim

Two roads follow the gorge. On the north side the Route des Crêtes loops out of La Palud-sur-Verdon past a string of belvederes, each one a sheer drop onto the river; on the south, the Corniche Sublime runs the other lip. Drive them slowly, stop often, and watch for the vultures — griffon vultures, reintroduced here, ride the thermals out of the canyon on most clear days.

“The river is the colour nothing natural should be, and it is entirely natural.”

From the notebook

II. The lake of Sainte-Croix

Where the gorge opens, the river feeds the Lac de Sainte-Croix, a vast glass-green reservoir held behind a dam. At the bridge below Galetas you can take a pedalo or a kayak up into the mouth of the canyon itself — the cliffs closing overhead, the water cold and clear, swimmers turning back where the current quickens.

III. Moustiers, hung with its star

At the western gate of the gorge, Moustiers-Sainte-Marie spills down a cleft in the cliff, a gold star strung on a chain between the two rock walls above it — a vow, the story goes, kept for centuries. One of the Plus Beaux Villages de France, it has made fine faïence pottery since the seventeenth century, and the workshops are still open along its steep lanes.

Plan the drive

Getting there
Best by car; La Palud-sur-Verdon and Moustiers make the two natural bases, about 2 h from Aix.
When to go
Late spring and September; the Route des Crêtes can close in winter, and July is busy on the water.
Where to stay
La Bastide de Moustiers, below the village, for the gorge by day and a quiet table at night.
