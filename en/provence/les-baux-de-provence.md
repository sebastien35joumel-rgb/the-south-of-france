Les Baux rides a spur of white rock in the heart of the **Alpilles**, a village and a ruined fortress so fused with the stone that from below you cannot tell where the cliff ends and the walls begin. It is one of the **Plus Beaux Villages de France**, and it gave its name to the mineral first found here — **bauxite**, the ore of aluminium.

## I. The fortress on the rock

The **Château des Baux** spreads across the top of the spur, a vast medieval ruin of towers, keep and rock-cut chambers with siege engines on the terraces and the whole plain of the Alpilles falling away below. Beneath it the village proper is a single climbing street of honey stone, Renaissance houses and small squares — best walked early, before the day fills it.

“You cannot tell where the cliff ends and the castle begins — they are the same white stone.”

## II. The Carrières de Lumières

Just below the village, the old **limestone quarries** — galleries cut in sheer white walls thirty feet high — have become the **Carrières de Lumières**, where a whole painter’s work is projected across floor, walls and ceiling to music. Van Gogh, Cézanne, Klimt: each year a new show fills the cavern with moving colour. It is the most extraordinary art experience in the South, and the coolest place to be on a hot day.

## III. The Alpilles around it

Les Baux sits at the heart of the **Alpilles**, the small dry limestone range of olive groves and garrigue that gives one of France’s finest olive oils, the **Vallée des Baux** AOC. **Saint-Rémy-de-Provence** is fifteen minutes north; the whole massif is laced with walking and cycling roads through pine and rock.

### Plan the _visit_

Getting there

About 30 min from Avignon or Arles by car; there is no train, and parking is below the village.

When to go

Spring and autumn; come at opening, as the village fills by late morning.

Where to stay

**Baumanière**, in the valley below, an old farm turned country hotel with a famous table.
