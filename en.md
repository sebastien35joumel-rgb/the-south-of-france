## Forty-eight hours on the _Îles d’Or_, where summer never ends.

Words — The Editors · Hyères, Var

There is a stretch of the Var coast where the mainland loosens its grip and three islands float just offshore — Porquerolles, Port-Cros, Le Levant. We took the first boat out of La Tour Fondue, a notebook, and no return ticket. What we found was the South as it was promised: pine, salt, and the long afternoon light that painters chase and never quite catch.

Crillon-le-Brave · Vaucluse

#### La Table de Castigno

Assignan · Hérault

A two-star table inside a wine-village resort, where Vincent Farges plays with the Languedoc’s bounty. The most quietly inventive kitchen west of Marseille.

#### Domaine de Trévallon

Saint-Étienne-du-Grès

Eloi Dürrbach’s mythical estate at the foot of the Alpilles. Forty years of biodynamic farming, and reds that age for thirty. Tastings by appointment.

Saint-Raphaël · Var

A 1950s seaside hotel rescued from kitsch — bare terrazzo, a saltwater pool cut into the rock, and the red Esterel falling straight into the blue.

Moustiers-Sainte-Marie · Alpes-de-Haute-Provence
